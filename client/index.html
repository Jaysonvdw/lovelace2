What is The Lovelace Project?

Augusta Ada King, Countess of Lovelace (10 December 1815 - 27 November 1852) is often regarded as the first computer programmer for her work on early analytical engines and algorithms. The Lovelace Project is our way of honouring this great pioneer in the early history of computers, by helping modern women to achieve their goals in setting up an online business or digital product. We endeavour to help non-technical, female, micro-entrepreneurs and freelancers to understand the full spectrum of their start-up, whether it be design, code, growth hacking or analytics, and everything in between, as well as ensuring long term success of the venture going forward.

How does the concept work?

This is our process, in a nutshell:

One of our consultants will meet with you to discuss your vision for the project, we'll sign an NDA and then perform a SWOT analysis. Even if you don't have an idea yet, we'll help you develop one!
We'll walk through product design with you covering all aspects of front-end design
We'll take care of all the complex back-end and database code, including API integrations
Once the product is closer to launch, we'll meet to discuss marketing channels, hosting, customer support and various other technicalities
